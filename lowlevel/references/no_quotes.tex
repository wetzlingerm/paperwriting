
\guideline[g:references:no_quotes]
    {Do not use direct quotes.}

\goodbadexample[{\cite[Sec.~I.A.]{Wetzlinger2023TAC}}]{
    Another work \highlightpart{``approximates the set of states reachable within time bound $T$ in a linear dynamical system to within an arbitrary error bound $\varepsilon$,''} but is limited to affine systems [41].
}{
    Another work \highlightpart{tunes the time step size by approximating the flow below a user-defined error bound} but is limited to affine systems [41].
}

\noindent
While direct quotes may be common in other research domains, they are practically unseen in computer science.
Instead of re-using a formulation verbatim, rephrase the content in your own words.
