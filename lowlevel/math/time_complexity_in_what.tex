
\guideline[g:math:time_space_complexity]
    {Use time/space complexities with explicit dependencies.}

\goodbadexample[{\cite[Sec.~VII.D.]{Wetzlinger2025TAC}}]{
    The evaluation of the scalable platoon benchmark demonstrates the polynomial \highlightpart{time complexity} of all our backward reachability algorithms, enabling the analysis of very high-dimensional linear systems.
}{
    The evaluation of the scalable platoon benchmark demonstrates the polynomial \highlightpart{time complexity in the state dimension} of all our backward reachability algorithms, enabling the analysis of very high-dimensional linear systems.
}

\noindent Often, the time/space complexity may be inferred from context.
However, the statement is incomplete without mentioning the variable with respect to which a certain behavior is exhibited.
This is particularly useful for readers who are skimming the text for this specific information.
