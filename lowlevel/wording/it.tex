
\guideline[g:wording:it]
    {Avoid using ``it'' or ``them'' as objects.}

\goodbadexample[{\cite[Sec.~IV.D.]{Wetzlinger2023TAC}}]{
    Finally, we combine \highlightpart{them} to obtain the following heuristic:
}{
    Finally, we combine \highlightpart{the two parts (40) and (41) describing the counteracting influences} to obtain the following heuristic:
}

\noindent
Using nondeterminate objects leads to vagueness, even when referring to a term mentioned directly beforehand, and there often is a more direct formulation.
