
\guideline{Refer to environments instead of introducing new names.}

\goodbadexample{
    \textbf{Algorithm 1.} \highlightpart{\textsc{RandomSimulation}} \\
    (...) \smallskip

    \highlightpart{Our proposed algorithm \textsc{RandomSimulation}} simulates the system for randomly selected initial states.
}{
    \textbf{Algorithm 1.} \highlightpart{Random simulation.} \\
    (...) \smallskip

    \highlightpart{Algorithm~1} simulates the system for randomly selected initial states.
}

\noindent The environment's name serves as an identifier, eliminating the need for an additional one.
Such identifiers seamlessly integrate into running text, as well as pseudocode and tables.
