\goodexample[{\cite[Sec.~VII.A.]{Wetzlinger2025TAC}}]{
    To showcase the general concept of our approach, we first consider the deliberately simple example of an electric circuit consisting of a resistance $\highlightpartmath{R = \SI{2}{\ohm}}$, a capacitor with capacity $\highlightpartmath{C = \SI{1.5}{\milli\farad}}$, and a coil with inductance $\highlightpartmath{L = \SI{2.5}{\milli\henry}}$:
    \begin{equation*}
        \begin{pmatrix} \dot{u}_C(t) \\ \dot{i}_L(t) \end{pmatrix}
        =
        \begin{pmatrix} -\frac{1}{RC} & \frac{1}{C} \\ -\frac{1}{L} & 0 \end{pmatrix}
        \begin{pmatrix} u_C(t) \\ i_L(t) \end{pmatrix}
        +
        \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ \frac{1}{L} \end{pmatrix} u_I(t)
    \end{equation*}
    where the state is defined by the voltage at the capacitor $u_C(t)$ and the current at the coil $i_L(t)$.
    The initial set is $\highlightpartmath{\mathcal{X}^0 = [1, 3]\SI{}{\volt} \times [3, 5] \SI{}{\ampere}}$, the input voltage to the circuit $u_I(t)$ is uncertain within the set $\highlightpartmath{\mathcal{U} = [-0.1, 0.1] \SI{}{\volt}}$, and the time horizon is $\highlightpartmath{t_{\text{end}} = \SI{2}{\second}}$.
}

\noindent
It is indispensable to provide all necessary information to let others replicate and verify your results.
Furthermore, any comparison between approaches requires full transparency regarding the example.
Providing the data can take on many forms---in the running text, more compactly in a table, by a reference, or even in a repeatability package.
